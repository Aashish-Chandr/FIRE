Emergency Fund Planning in India

An emergency fund is a financial safety net to handle unexpected situations without disturbing long-term investments.

BASIC RULE:
- Salaried individuals: 6 months of expenses
- Self-employed/freelancers: 12 months of expenses

WHERE TO KEEP:
- High-yield savings accounts (Kotak 811, Fi Money, IDFC First Bank offer ~6–7%)
- Liquid mutual funds
- Never invest emergency funds in stock market or locked instruments

---

JOB LOSS EMERGENCY:
- Emergency fund must cover EMIs, rent, food, and utilities for at least 6 months
- Pause all SIPs except EPF during job loss
- Resume investments once income is restored
- Withdraw EPF partially if unemployment exceeds 2 months (via EPFO portal)
- Avoid withdrawing mutual funds unless emergency fund is exhausted
- Use job loss period for upskilling (NPTEL, Coursera audit, Google certificates)

---

MEDICAL EMERGENCY:
- Major cause of debt in India
- Maintain health insurance before starting FIRE
- Hospitalization cost: ₹2–10 lakhs
- Keep ₹1–2 lakhs liquid for non-covered expenses
- Critical illness cost: ₹10–30 lakhs
- Consider critical illness rider in term insurance
- Ayushman Bharat covers up to ₹5 lakhs (check eligibility)

---

MARRIAGE EXPENSES (NOT AN EMERGENCY):
- Typical cost: ₹5–50 lakhs
- Plan separately using RD or debt mutual funds
- Do not use emergency fund
- If financial stress occurs:
  - Reduce lifestyle expenses
  - Pause non-essential investments
- Gold can be used as collateral for loans

---

VEHICLE EMERGENCY:
- Keep ₹50,000–₹1,00,000 buffer
- Maintain comprehensive vehicle insurance

---

HOME REPAIR EMERGENCY:
- Repair cost: ₹1–5 lakhs
- Keep extra ₹1–2 lakhs beyond standard emergency fund
- Home insurance covers fire, flood, structural damage
- Premium: ₹2,000–₹5,000 per year

---

FAMILY DEPENDENCY EMERGENCY:
- Sole earners face sudden expense spikes
- Parents' medical emergencies common (age 30–45)
- Buy senior citizen health insurance for parents
- Cost: ₹15,000–₹40,000 annually

---

BUILDING YOUR EMERGENCY FUND:
1. Start with ₹50,000 mini emergency fund
2. Build to 3 months expenses
3. Then expand to 6–12 months
4. Automate 10% salary transfer on salary day
5. After completion, redirect this amount to investments

IMPORTANT RULES:
- Do not use emergency fund for planned expenses
- Do not lock emergency funds in fixed deposits
- Use only liquid or ultra-short-term instruments
- Emergency fund = financial survival tool, not investment
